Marketplace · Allegro · Otomoto

Helping people make better decisions when choosing a vehicle.

Poland's largest vehicle marketplace — where everyday buyers needed to move from “what am I looking for?” to a purchase they could stand behind.

My role: Design lead

The Problem

Discovery broke down early: filters rewarded precision buyers didn't have yet, mobile sessions ended in fatigue, and shortlists were built in tabs and memory — not in the product. Even promising listings stalled on trust — no way to know if a car was truly accident-free or hiding defects the ad wouldn't mention. Financing and verification lived outside the journey, so momentum died before contact.

Key Insights

Buyers think in use cases — “family car”, “city commute” — not in spec sheets they haven't chosen yet.

Believing the listing and believing the car are different problems — history and condition are where doubt actually lives.

Re-entering the same criteria kills momentum — people expect search to remember what they already decided.

Affordability is part of the decision — financing belongs in the flow, not as a separate errand after the fact.

Solution

A modern marketplace shaped around what buyers asked for — persistent search, in-context verification and installment planning — as one path from intent to purchase.

User outcomes / impact

Improved conversion A/B tests showed a higher shortlist-to-contact rate after the redesigned discovery flow.
Scale Rolled out to millions of monthly active buyers nationwide.
Higher financing engagement Installment calculators and on-site financing saw measurably higher uptake after launch.
More expert verification Professional history and condition checks grew as buyers verified offers before reaching out to sellers.
