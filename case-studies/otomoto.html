Helping people make better decisions when choosing a vehicle.

Discovery built around intent — so buyers compare listings with confidence, not filter fatigue.

Search & Discovery — screenshot placeholder

The Problem

Millions of listings, but buyers struggled to find vehicles that matched their real needs — not just their search filters. Discovery was filter-driven, not intent-driven. The business needed higher engagement and conversion without adding complexity for casual buyers. The constraint: mobile-first audience, seconds to decide, millions of listings.

How We Understood the Problem

01

Understand Business

Business context
  • Connected design goals to listing engagement and lead quality.
  • Prioritized buyer discovery over seller tooling for this phase.
  • Aligned mobile and web teams on one discovery model.

Key insight Conversion follows confidence — buyers need to trust results, not see more results.

02

Learn from Users

User research
  • Buyers refined searches endlessly without feeling closer to a decision.
  • Comparison was manual — open tabs, screenshots, memory.
  • Mobile users abandoned when filters felt like work.

Key insight Buyers don't want more filters — they want fewer wrong results.

03

Shape the Experience

Experience design
  • Search results prioritized relevance signals over raw count.
  • Comparison built into the flow — not a separate feature.
  • Mobile discovery reduced steps from intent to shortlist.

Key insight Discovery is a decision funnel, not a search engine.

04

Validate

Validation
  • A/B tests showed improved shortlist-to-contact conversion.
  • Mobile sessions grew longer with fewer filter interactions.
  • User feedback highlighted "found it faster" as top theme.

Key insight Less searching, more deciding — validated at scale.

Read more — research & validation details

Diary studies with car buyers revealed decision anxiety — not lack of options. Card sorting showed buyers think in use cases ("family car", "city commute") not spec sheets. Prototype testing on mobile confirmed simplified filter paths outperformed comprehensive filter panels.

Key Design Decisions

Relevance over comprehensiveness

Alternatives
More filters, more results, more sorting options.
Tradeoffs
Power users lose granular control upfront.
Decision
Prioritize best matches — expose advanced filters progressively.

Impact

Scale Used by millions of buyers monthly across Poland.
Business impact Improved engagement and shortlist-to-contact conversion.

Reflection

At marketplace scale, small friction compounds into massive drop-off. I'd test intent-based entry points earlier — buyers arrive with a use case, not a spec sheet.
