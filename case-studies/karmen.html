Project Overview

A walkthrough by Mike Spisak — what we built, how KARMEN works, and the impact it delivered at IBM Call for Code.

The Problem

During the COVID-19 pandemic, phishing attacks increased dramatically. Many users — especially elderly people and non-technical users — struggled to distinguish legitimate messages from scams.

The challenge was to design an experience that transformed complex cybersecurity analysis into simple, confident decisions anyone could understand — without exposing technical threat intelligence or overwhelming people with security jargon.

How We Understood the Problem

01

Understand Business

  • Framed the product around a real human crisis — not a technology demo.
  • Prioritized social impact for vulnerable users during the pandemic.
  • Scoped the MVP to verify → understand → act, within an eight-week sprint.

Key insight The product wins when ordinary people can decide — not when security experts understand the data.

02

Learn from Users

  • Elderly and non-technical users were most exposed — and least equipped to evaluate risk.
  • Pasting text or uploading a screenshot was the most natural entry point.
  • Users needed guidance on what to do — not just a technical verdict.

Key insight Accessibility and confidence matter as much as detection accuracy.

03

Shape the Experience

  • Designed a Progressive Web App for cross-platform reach without app store friction.
  • Translated security signals into plain recommendations — hiding complexity behind clarity.
  • Built a high-contrast verdict screen that drives decisive action.

Key insight AI should simplify the decision — not showcase the technology.

04

Validate

  • Validated flows with non-technical participants for real-world accessibility.
  • Prototype testing confirmed users understood verdicts and next steps.
  • Won IBM Call for Code — recognized for product clarity and social impact.

Key insight Social impact and product thinking convinced the jury — not the AI alone.

Read more — research & validation details

User research surfaced a consistent pattern: people either delete suspicious messages out of fear, or click out of curiosity — rarely with confidence. Testing with elderly and non-technical participants showed that explaining what to do and why mattered more than exposing threat intelligence feeds. Accessibility decisions — large type, high contrast, plain language — were validated early, not added as an afterthought.

Solution

Verified suspicious messages in seconds.

KARMEN is an AI-powered Progressive Web App that helps users verify suspicious emails, SMS messages and screenshots — from any device, without installing an app.

Translated AI into human decisions.

Instead of exposing technical threat intelligence, KARMEN translates security signals into clear recommendations that ordinary users can understand and act upon.

Made accessibility part of the product — not a feature.

High-contrast verdicts, plain-language guidance and one-tap reporting — designed so elderly and non-technical users could make confident decisions.

Key Design Decisions

Recommendations over raw threat intelligence

Alternatives
Expose technical feeds, IP reputation and NLP scores.
Tradeoffs
Less visibility into the security engine for power users.
Decision
Translate signals into plain guidance — the product is the decision, not the data.

Accessibility-first for vulnerable users

Alternatives
Security-first UI optimized for technical audiences.
Tradeoffs
Less density of technical detail on screen.
Decision
Design for elderly and non-technical users from day one — high contrast, clear language, obvious next steps.

Progressive Web App over native install

Alternatives
Native iOS and Android apps with full platform features.
Tradeoffs
Limited access to some device-level integrations.
Decision
PWA for instant cross-platform access — critical during a pandemic when people needed help immediately.
