Consumer Security · Karmen

Helping people make better decisions about suspicious messages.

AI-powered SMS spam detection that gives people an instant read on risk — so they know whether to trust a message.

🏆 Call For Code 2020 Winner IBM Garage Project

The Problem

SMS spam is a growing global threat — and most people have no way to verify whether a message is safe or report it when it's not. They need a simple, intelligent solution to understand, validate and act in real time. The constraint: eight weeks, a seven-person team, and technology that had to earn trust instantly.

How We Understood the Problem

01

Understand Business

  • Aligned the team on real-world impact as the primary success metric.
  • Scoped MVP to validate → understand → act — not a full security suite.
  • Positioned Watson AI as invisible power behind visible clarity.

Key insight Winning products solve a felt problem — not a technical brief.

02

Learn from Users

  • People receive suspicious messages daily but don't know who to trust.
  • Pasting text or uploading a screenshot was the natural entry point.
  • Users needed guidance on what to do — not just a verdict.

Key insight Doubt is the enemy — clarity and next steps are the product.

03

Shape the Experience

  • Four-step model: Validate → Analyze → Understand → Take action.
  • Analysis progress made complex AI feel transparent and trustworthy.
  • High-contrast verdict screen drove confident decisions.

Key insight Show the machine thinking — users trust what they can see.

04

Validate

  • 95% spam detection accuracy validated against real threat data.
  • Prototype playback confirmed users understood the verdict flow.
  • Call For Code jury recognized real-world impact and design clarity.

Key insight Validation proved trust — not just technology.

Read more — research & validation details

User research surfaced a pattern: people delete suspicious messages out of fear, or click out of curiosity — rarely with confidence. Prototype testing showed that explaining why something is spam mattered as much as the verdict itself. The analysis screen with visible progress bars for NLP, threat feeds and IP reputation consistently increased trust scores in playback sessions.

Key Design Decisions

Transparent analysis over instant verdict

Alternatives
Instant result with no progress or explanation.
Tradeoffs
Slightly longer wait — but significantly higher trust.
Decision
Show analysis progress — users trust what they can follow.

Guidance + reporting over verdict-only

Alternatives
Simple spam/not-spam label with no next steps.
Tradeoffs
More screen real estate on the result view.
Decision
Every verdict includes what to do and why — plus one-tap report.

Reflection

Karmen taught me that the hardest part of AI products isn't the model — it's earning trust in three seconds. If I rebuilt it today, I'd push the "why" explanation even earlier in the flow. Eight weeks forced clarity we might have lost with more time.
