Cybersecurity · AI · KARMEN

Helping people recognize phishing before they become victims.

An AI-powered Progressive Web App that translates complex cybersecurity analysis into clear recommendations ordinary users can act upon — with accessibility at the center.

My role: Design lead

🏆 IBM Call for Code Winner AI Cybersecurity Accessibility Progressive Web App Open Source

The Problem

During the COVID-19 pandemic, phishing attacks increased dramatically. Elderly and non-technical users struggled to distinguish legitimate messages from scams — and security tools exposed threat intelligence they couldn't act on. The challenge was to turn complex analysis into simple, confident decisions anyone could understand.

Key Insights

The product wins when ordinary people can decide — not when security experts understand the data.

Accessibility and confidence matter as much as detection accuracy for vulnerable users.

Pasting text or uploading a screenshot was the most natural entry point — not configuring security tools.

AI should simplify the decision — not showcase the technology behind it.

Solution

Verify suspicious messages in seconds.

Users paste text or upload a screenshot from any device — no app install, no security jargon to get started.

Translate AI into human decisions.

Instead of exposing threat intelligence, KARMEN turns security signals into plain recommendations users can understand and act on.

Make accessibility part of the product — not a feature.

High-contrast verdicts, plain-language guidance and one-tap reporting — designed so elderly and non-technical users make confident decisions.

User outcomes / impact

🏆 IBM Call for Code Winner Recognized for real-world impact, product clarity and social relevance during the pandemic.
Confident decisions for non-technical users Complex security analysis simplified into verdicts and next steps anyone could follow.
Cross-platform Progressive Web App Accessible from any device — critical when people needed help immediately, without an app store.
Released as open source Shared on GitHub so the community can extend protection against cyber scams to more people.
