KARMEN

Helping people spot phishing
before it's too late

An AI-powered Progressive Web App that translates complex cybersecurity analysis into clear recommendations ordinary users can act upon — with accessibility at the center.

My role: Design lead

The Problem

During the COVID-19 pandemic, phishing attacks increased dramatically.
Elderly and non-technical users struggled to distinguish legitimate messages from scams — and security tools exposed threat intelligence they couldn't act on.
The challenge was to turn complex analysis into simple, confident decisions anyone could understand.

Key Insights

  • The product wins when ordinary people can decide — not when security experts understand the data.
  • Accessibility and confidence matter as much as detection accuracy for vulnerable users.
  • Pasting text or uploading a screenshot was the most natural entry point — not configuring security tools.
  • AI should simplify the decision — not showcase the technology behind it.

Solution

Free, open source protection anyone can use.

We built a Progressive Web App that is free and open source — simple enough for anyone to pick up, designed to protect ordinary people most at risk from phishing and cyber scams.

Validate suspicious messages in seconds.

Paste text or upload a screenshot from any device — no app store, no security jargon. The entry point had to feel as natural as sharing a message you are unsure about.

Analysis users can follow — not fear.

Threat intelligence runs behind the scenes while users see clear, human-readable progress — X-Force data and reputation checks translated into steps that build trust, not confusion.

Verdicts that drive action.

High-contrast results, plain-language guidance and one-tap reporting — so elderly and non-technical users know exactly what to do when a message is spam.

Project Overview

A walkthrough by Mike Spisak — what we built, how KARMEN works, and the impact it delivered at IBM Call for Code.

User outcomes / impact

  • 🏆 IBM Call for Code 2020 Winner Selected from 2,200+ projects in IBM’s internal challenge — 6,500 participants across the company — before winning Call for Code for real-world impact, product clarity and social relevance during the pandemic.
  • Everyday users felt like experts Complex security analysis became clear verdicts and next steps anyone could follow — so people trusted their own judgment on suspicious messages, not just a warning. Quick scenario-based usability checks made that easy to validate.
  • Cross-platform Progressive Web App Accessible from any device — critical when people needed help immediately, without an app store.
  • Released as open source Shared on GitHub so the community can extend protection against cyber scams to more people.
