Observability · Elastic SLO

Helping SREs act before users feel the impact.

A reliability module in Elastic Observability that gives SRE teams and on-call engineers one view of which services are healthy, at risk, or breached — so they act on error budgets before users notice.

My role: Design lead

The Problem

SRE teams tracked dozens of services but couldn't answer a simple question: are we meeting our reliability commitments? SLO data lived across dashboards, alerts and spreadsheets — disconnected from daily decisions. On-call engineers spent minutes assembling context that should take seconds.

Key Insights

SLOs only matter when they change what teams do Monday morning — not when they're configured once and forgotten.

Monitoring isn't the problem — knowing when to act on error budget burn rate is.

SREs preferred status-at-a-glance over detailed charts upfront — depth on demand, not by default.

Show the health story first — let engineers choose how deep to drill into root cause.

Solution

See service health at a glance.

One SLO overview shows which services are healthy or violated — replacing scattered metric checks across dashboards and alerts.

Assess error budgets faster.

Burn rate, budget remaining and SLI trends answer "how fast are we consuming budget?" — without manual calculation or exports.

Create SLOs where engineers already work.

SLO creation lives in APM service actions — engineers define reliability targets from the service context they monitor daily.

Embed SLO health in any dashboard.

Reusable SLO widgets — from compact dependency lists to full cards — bring reliability status into the views teams already use.

User outcomes / impact

Faster time-to-assessment Prototype testing cut time-to-assessment by half in lab sessions with SRE teams.
Daily reliability check Pilot customers adopted the SLO overview as their starting point for every shift.
Global rollout Shipped as core SLO experience in Elastic Observability for customers worldwide.
Positive customer feedback SRE teams valued status-first design over metrics-heavy dashboards they had to interpret.
