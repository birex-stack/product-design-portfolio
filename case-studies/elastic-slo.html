The Problem

SRE teams tracked dozens of services but couldn't answer a simple question: are we meeting our reliability commitments? SLO data existed across dashboards, alerts and spreadsheets — disconnected from daily decisions. The business needed a single source of truth for service health. The constraint: integrate with existing Elastic Observability workflows without adding configuration overhead.

How We Understood the Problem

01

Understand Business

Business context
  • Aligned product and engineering on SLO as a reliability contract.
  • Reduced scope to actionable SLO states — healthy, at risk, breached.
  • Connected design goals to error budget consumption metrics.

Key insight SLOs only matter when they change what teams do Monday morning.

02

Learn from Users

User research
  • SREs configured SLOs but rarely returned to check them.
  • On-call engineers needed status in seconds, not minutes.
  • Error budget burn rate was the signal that drove action.

Key insight Monitoring isn't the problem — knowing when to act is.

03

Shape the Experience

Experience design
  • Service-level overview replaced fragmented per-metric views.
  • Burn rate visualization made budget depletion intuitive.
  • Drill-down preserved context from overview to root cause.

Key insight Show the health story first — let engineers choose the depth.

04

Validate

Validation
  • Prototype testing cut time-to-assessment by half in lab sessions.
  • SREs preferred status-at-a-glance over detailed charts upfront.
  • Pilot customers adopted SLO overview as daily starting point.

Key insight Validation confirmed: less data upfront, faster decisions.

Key Design Decisions

Status-first over metrics-first

Alternatives
Traditional metrics dashboard with SLO as one panel.
Tradeoffs
Less granular data visible initially.
Decision
Lead with SLO health status — metrics available on drill-down.

Impact

Global rollout Shipped to Elastic Observability customers worldwide.
Positive customer feedback SRE teams adopted SLO overview as daily reliability check.

Reflection

The biggest surprise was how little SREs cared about SLO setup — they wanted ongoing awareness. I'd invest more in alert-to-SLO linking earlier, since that's where action actually happens.
