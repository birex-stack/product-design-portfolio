The Problem

Analysts detected anomalies across logs, metrics and alerts — but turning isolated signals into coherent investigations was manual and inconsistent. Cases were tracked in spreadsheets, chat threads and ticket systems. The business needed a native investigation workflow inside Elastic. The constraint: fit existing analyst habits without forcing a new toolchain.

How We Understood the Problem

01

Understand Business

Business context
  • Positioned Cases as the investigation hub within Elastic.
  • Aligned security and ops teams on shared case lifecycle.
  • Scoped MVP to create, enrich and close — not full SOAR.

Key insight Cases succeed when they reduce context-switching, not add process.

02

Learn from Users

User research
  • Analysts rebuilt context manually every time they switched tools.
  • Similar past cases were the most valued — and hardest to find.
  • Collaboration happened in side channels, invisible to the case.

Key insight Investigations are memory problems before they're data problems.

03

Shape the Experience

Experience design
  • One case aggregates alerts, events and analyst notes.
  • Timeline view shows investigation progression clearly.
  • Similar cases surfaced proactively during investigation.

Key insight A case is a container for context — not a ticket with a title.

04

Validate

Validation
  • Playback sessions showed faster case creation from alerts.
  • Analysts trusted the timeline over external note-taking.
  • Pilot teams reduced tool-switching during investigations.

Key insight Adoption followed when Cases lived where analysts already worked.

Read more — research & validation details

Activity tracking showed analysts averaged 14 context switches per investigation. Interviews revealed "similar cases" were found through memory and Slack — not search. Storyboard sessions validated that timeline-first layout matched how analysts narrate incidents to colleagues.

Key Design Decisions

Timeline-first over list-first

Alternatives
Tabbed interface with separate alert, event and note panels.
Tradeoffs
Less flexible for power users who prefer raw lists.
Decision
Timeline as primary view — matches how analysts tell the story.

Impact

Enterprise adoption Adopted by security teams within Elastic customer base.
Engineering impact Unified investigation model across Elastic Security modules.

Reflection

Analysts didn't want a new tool — they wanted less fragmentation. Today I'd push harder on collaborative features earlier, since investigations are team sports even when they look individual.
