Enterprise Security · AI · IBM XFTM

From alert noise to trusted triage.

IBM XFTM: A triage workflow built around ATDS — IBM's Advanced Threat Disposition System — turning trusted AI/ML scoring into actionable alerts analysts can close, escalate or automate with confidence.

My role: Design lead, Managing consultant

The Problem

Security teams were drowning in alerts. Triage analysts had access to vast amounts of data but lacked a clear path from signal to decision. Behind the scenes, ATDS — IBM's AI/ML disposition engine — could analyze alerts and recommend actions with automation. The design challenge was making those recommendations trusted, explainable and fast enough for real investigations.

The business needed faster disposition — close false positives or escalate real threats — without sacrificing auditability. The constraint: enterprise-grade complexity, analyst-grade speed, and AI that analysts would actually rely on. The product wins when analysts decide — not when they collect more data.

Design Process

Guided by IBM Enterprise Design Thinking — a shared language to align faster, focus on what matters, and deliver with empathy and speed. One continuous flow from business context to shipped product.

  1. 01

    Understand business requirements

    Mapped the project background with security operations leadership and product — scoped around ATDS, MSIEM triage goals, and what “faster disposition” means for IBM Security and its clients.

  2. 02

    Understand the user

    Mapped five archetypes across IBM SOC and client environments — triage analysts, threat hunters, account managers and client security leaders — shaping priorities for queue scanning, disposition speed and trust in ATDS. The highest drop-off sat between queue scan and case open, not inside the investigation itself.

    Shadowing sessions in SOC
    To understand their day-to-day work, jobs to be done.
    Interviews with analysts
    To understand pain points and potential improvements.
    Activity tracking
    To analyze their usage of current toolset — reality check.

  3. 03

    Ideate with the team

    Ran brainstorms and playback sessions with designers, product managers, engineers and SOC stakeholders — exploring queue models, two-view triage, ATDS explainability and automation boundaries before committing to a direction.

  4. 04

    Experience-based Roadmap

    Stress-tested every horizon against Raphael — our Main Hill archetype — so each release advanced one shared outcome, not a separate feature lane.

    Short term
    Scan the cases queue, filter by client and severity, and open the right offense without parsing raw event data.
    Mid term
    Orient on a case with magnitude and IP context, correlate related alerts, and act on ATDS guidance with explainable risk scoring.
    Long term
    Complete a high-quality investigation for any alert within 2 minutes — close, escalate or automate with confidence.

    If we succeed, analysts dispose with context upfront — not after rebuilding it across tabs, tickets and memory.

  5. 05

    Mid-fidelity design

    Shaped the to-be scenario for the Threat Analyst alert investigation flow — queue scan, case overview, correlation and ATDS disposition as one coherent decision model, aligned to Carbon Design System patterns.

  6. 06

    Playback, validate, iterate

    Presented concepts to stakeholders and triage analysts across client groups. Collected feedback, iterated on risk-led layout and ATDS trust patterns — pilot playback confirmed progressive disclosure of IP intelligence outperformed surfacing everything at once — and reached common agreement on version one scope.

  7. 07

    Hi-fi mockups & prototype

    Delivered high-fidelity mockups and an interactive prototype for end-to-end validation — from cases queue through ATDS disposition — ready for engineering handoff.

  8. 08

    Ship, measure, learn

    Rolled out within the IBM Security enterprise portfolio. Measured time-to-disposition and analyst confidence, and kept a continuous feedback loop with SOC teams to refine triage defaults and ATDS thresholds post-launch.

Solution

Built on what triage analysts and IBM Security leadership needed — faster disposition without sacrificing auditability, the right context before commitment, and ATDS guidance trusted enough to act on.

Reduced cognitive load in the queue.

Severity, assignment and client group visible at a glance — analysts scan without parsing raw event data. ATDS-driven prioritization highlights which offenses need attention now.

Case overview at a glance.

Magnitude, rules, events and IP intelligence surfaced upfront — analysts orient on an Exploit case without drilling into raw logs.

Correlate before you commit.

Related alerts grouped by correlated indicators — analysts review matches and merge offenses into the active case in one step.

ATDS guidance you can act on.

Explainable AI scoring with risk thresholds and disposition context — analysts close, escalate or associate with a related case, with full control over every decision.

Key Design Decisions

  • One tool instead of eight — the right context at the right moment, curated for each step of the investigation, not scattered across tabs.
  • Explainable ATDS guidance — analysts stay in control, AI handles the heavy lifting.
  • Risk-led layout — lead with assessment, raw data on demand.
  • Explicit disposition paths — associate, escalate or dismiss, every outcome analyst-owned and auditable.
