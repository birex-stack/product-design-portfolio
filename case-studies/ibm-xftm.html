The Problem

Security teams were drowning in alerts. Triage analysts had access to vast amounts of data but lacked a clear path from signal to decision. Behind the scenes, ATDS — IBM's AI/ML disposition engine — could analyze alerts and recommend actions with automation. The design challenge was making those recommendations trusted, explainable and fast enough for real investigations.

The business needed faster disposition — close false positives or escalate real threats — without sacrificing auditability. The constraint: enterprise-grade complexity, analyst-grade speed, and AI that analysts would actually rely on.

How We Understood the Problem

01

Understand Business

  • Aligned goals across security, support and product teams.
  • Scoped the product around ATDS — trusted AI/ML disposition with selectable automation.
  • Defined success as time-to-disposition, not feature count.

Key insight The product wins when analysts decide — not when they investigate.

02

Learn from Users

  • Analysts spent more time collecting data than making decisions.
  • Most disposition calls happened in a narrow time window.
  • Contextual data mattered more than comprehensive data.

Key insight Speed comes from the right context upfront — not more tabs.

03

Shape the Experience

  • Two-view model: scan the queue, then commit to a case.
  • ATDS confidence scoring and risk signals unified in one disposition flow.
  • Escalation and automation as deliberate, auditable actions.

Key insight Every screen must earn its place in the decision chain.

04

Validate

  • Prototypes confirmed risk-led layout reduced hesitation.
  • Analysts closed false positives with more confidence.
  • Carbon Design System alignment accelerated delivery.

Key insight Validation proved the model — not individual UI components.

Read more — research & validation details

Shadowing sessions revealed analysts switching between six tools before making a single disposition call. Interviews with Tier 1 and Tier 2 teams surfaced different mental models of "done." Activity tracking showed the highest drop-off happened between queue scan and case open — not inside the investigation itself.

Pilot playback sessions with three client groups validated that progressive disclosure of IP intelligence outperformed showing everything at once.

Solution

Reduced cognitive load in the queue.

Severity, assignment and client group visible at a glance — analysts scan without parsing raw event data.

Surfaced ATDS-driven prioritization.

AI/ML scoring from ATDS highlighted which alerts needed attention now — turning automation-ready signals into an actionable queue, not noise.

Made trusted AI recommendations actionable.

Case detail presented ATDS disposition guidance with confidence, risk context and similar cases — so analysts could close, escalate or automate with explainable AI support.

Key Design Decisions

Two views instead of one dashboard

Alternatives
Single unified dashboard with drill-down panels.
Tradeoffs
More navigation steps, but clearer mental model per task.
Decision
Separate queue scanning from case investigation — matching how analysts actually work.

Trusted AI guidance over black-box automation

Alternatives
Auto-close or auto-escalate alerts without analyst-visible reasoning.
Tradeoffs
More UI space for confidence scoring and disposition context.
Decision
Surface ATDS recommendations with explainability — analysts stay in control, AI handles the heavy lifting.

Risk-led layout over data-led layout

Alternatives
Chronological event stream as the primary view.
Tradeoffs
Less raw data visible upfront, but faster disposition calls.
Decision
Lead with risk assessment — raw data available on demand, not by default.
